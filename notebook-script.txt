HIREIQ is a full-stack AI-powered career operating system and recruitment intelligence platform built to bring the entire job-readiness journey into one premium workspace: instead of making a user jump between a resume checker, a resume builder, a roadmap generator, a mock interview app, a company research tool, a portfolio site maker, and a separate admin CRM, the app combines all of those surfaces into a single React/Vite product backed by Express, MongoDB/Mongoose, JWT authentication, Zustand state, Gemini AI, Tailwind/CSS variables, Framer Motion, and a polished app-shell experience. The core idea is that a student, fresher, working professional, or career switcher can register, log in, upload or create a resume, analyze it for ATS quality, extract their latest career context, reuse that context across every AI feature, plan what to learn next, practice interviews, research companies, study real leaders, build a portfolio, and manage their personal profile without repeating the same information again and again. The public side of the product works as the trust and conversion layer: Home introduces the platform as a career coach OS, About explains the mission of bridging talent and opportunity, Pricing presents plan tiers and upgrades, Contact captures leads directly into the backend, and Privacy Policy gives the basic data-handling promise. The authentication layer includes register, login, forgot-password, and reset-password flows, with server-side password hashing through bcrypt, JWT session tokens, initial admin bootstrapping from environment variables, and protected route separation between normal users and admins. Once signed in, the user enters a dedicated app layout with navigation for Dashboard, Resume Analyzer, Resume Maker, Mock Interview, Career Path, Skills Lab, AI Roadmap, Portfolio Builder, Company Insights, Networking, Profile, Settings, and History, making the product feel like a single professional command center rather than a set of disconnected pages. The Dashboard is the user’s starting cockpit, meant to summarize progress, shortcuts, and career actions. Resume Analyzer is the diagnostic engine: it parses user files through the local file parser, sends resume text or image context to Gemini through the AI service, returns ATS-style scoring, strengths, weaknesses, missing keywords, formatting advice, experience and skill scoring, and improvement recommendations, and then feeds useful resume information into the global resume pipeline store. Resume Maker is the creation surface, with resume architect types, preview components, constants, and templates such as Modern Executive, Creative Minimalist, and Power Sidebar, letting users assemble a cleaner resume and visually inspect it before export-like usage. The resume pipeline store is important because it saves the latest resume text, role, skills, education, and presence flag so features like Skills Lab, Career Path, Mock Interview, AI Roadmap, and Company Insights can personalize themselves without asking the user to start from zero each time. Mock Interview turns a target role and profile into interview preparation, simulated questions, coaching, and persisted interview records. Career Path helps users reason about career direction, target roles, skill gaps, and next steps. Skills Lab and Subject Lab provide assessment-like learning and practice surfaces where users can focus on domain skills and subject-specific improvement. AI Roadmap converts user education, skills, interests, location, and goals into a personalized learning route with recommended domains, project ideas, skill sequencing, and career outcomes. Portfolio Builder helps the user package projects, skills, achievements, and professional identity into a presentable portfolio narrative, solving the common problem that job seekers often have skills but no polished evidence. Company Insights is the market-intelligence layer: it combines static company data with a server-side live trend parser in server.ts that refreshes market signals, salary benchmarks, company growth status, and hiring trends from public sources, caches them in data/company-trends.json, and exposes them through an API so the frontend can display company readiness, interview focus, culture clues, project ideas, and market movement. Networking is the research and outreach layer: it does not pretend that famous CEOs are available for direct chat; instead it presents verified public profiles of major technology and entrepreneurial leaders like Satya Nadella, Sundar Pichai, Jensen Huang, Tim Cook, Andy Jassy, Elon Musk, Sam Altman, Deepinder Goyal, Bhavish Aggarwal, Vijay Shekhar Sharma, Falguni Nayar, and Harshil Mathur, with their roles, companies, social/profile references, expertises, career milestones, recent activities, public statement angles, boards and impact work, risk notes, and official source links, plus an outreach assistant that helps users draft respectful questions, study leadership lessons, and connect their own goals to real public examples. Profile management makes the account feel personal and persistent: users can edit full name, email ID, phone, location, target role, skills, preferred AI model, and profile picture, with the profile picture compressed in-browser into a small square JPEG data URL before saving so server/database space is protected. User Settings covers security and preferences, including a local MFA-style toggle, direct password update with current-password verification, and local data/session clearing. The AI layer is centralized in src/services/aiService.ts, where Gemini calls use a model cascade and the user’s preferred model is tried first, allowing a user to choose speed, balance, or precision while retaining fallback safety if a model is unavailable, rate-limited, or unsupported. The backend in server.ts is the main full-stack spine: it configures Express, CORS, Helmet, JSON body limits, MongoDB connection behavior, JWT authentication middleware, auth routes, password reset routes, user profile routes, password-update routes, resume routes, interview routes, skills lab result routes, career path result routes, enrollment routes, contact lead capture, admin stats, admin user management, admin lead management, email reply routes, campaign and email-template routes, global admin settings, company-insight trend APIs, and Vite middleware for development. The database models represent the app’s business objects: User stores auth identity, admin role, plan, preferred model, avatar, profile strength, phone, location, target role, skills, and timestamps; Resume stores user-created or analyzed resume data; Interview stores interview records; Lead captures contact and CRM-style inquiries; SkillsLabResult and CareerPathResult persist AI or assessment output; Enrollment tracks course/user participation; Campaign and EmailTemplate power admin communication workflows; Settings stores global platform configuration such as analysis model, system prompt, brand color, and platform name. The admin side is the operations layer for the business running HIREIQ: Admin Dashboard gives platform statistics and recent lead visibility, Users lets admins search and manage accounts, Leads and Contacts support CRM workflows, Email Center manages replies, campaigns, and templates, and Admin Settings exposes AI/model/system prompt and branding controls so the operator can tune the platform without editing code. The design language is dark, premium, futuristic, and dense enough for a real productivity tool, using glass panels, tight cards, animated route transitions, Lucide icons, responsive layouts, theme and sound toggles, notification UI, back buttons, progress visuals, and app/sidebar shells that create a professional SaaS feeling. The app’s main product promise is continuity: a resume is not just a document upload, it becomes context for skills, interviews, roadmap, portfolio, and company matching; a profile is not just settings, it becomes personalization for every AI feature; admin data is not just stored, it becomes a lightweight CRM for leads, replies, and growth; and market data is not just a static table, it becomes live career intelligence. The target users are students preparing for internships, freshers preparing for first jobs, professionals switching roles, Indian startup and tech-career aspirants, bootcamp-style learners, and career coaches or institutes who want an admin-managed platform for users and leads. The strongest future version of HIREIQ would add real resume versioning, a job application tracker, calendar-based interview scheduling, richer portfolio publishing, recruiter-facing candidate shortlists, payment/subscription enforcement, email sending through a provider instead of console-only reset links, deeper analytics dashboards, proper file/object storage for avatars and resumes, per-feature usage limits, stronger audit logs, more robust AI safety and prompt management, role-based admin permissions, vector search over user career history, and live integrations with job boards, LinkedIn-style profile import, GitHub project import, and learning platforms. In short, HIREIQ is intended to be a complete AI career coach OS: public site for acquisition, protected user workspace for career execution, AI services for intelligence, persistent stores for continuity, admin CRM for business operations, and a polished interface that turns career planning from scattered anxiety into a guided, measurable, and personalized workflow.
